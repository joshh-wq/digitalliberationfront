Social & Communication

The Problem

Every major social platform is owned by a billionaire who has bent the knee to authoritarianism. Meta gutted fact-checking. X became a right-wing megaphone. TikTok's fate is decided by politicians, not users. Our social lives are controlled by people who don't share our values—and we have zero say in how these platforms work.

What We're Building

User-owned social platforms structured as cooperative corporations. Every user is a member-owner. Major decisions—moderation policy, algorithm design, data practices—are voted on democratically. The platform can never be sold to a billionaire because the members own it.

What Already Exists

Bluesky

Decentralized, open protocol. Already millions of users. Not a co-op (yet), but its open protocol means no single entity controls it.

Mastodon

Federated social network. Community-run instances. Proves decentralized social media works at scale.

Signal

Nonprofit encrypted messaging. 40M+ users. Proves you don't need ads or data harvesting to build great communication tools.

Pixelfed

Decentralized Instagram alternative. No algorithm, no ads, no Meta. Photo sharing the way it should be.

What Still Needs Building

  • A co-op social platform that's as polished and intuitive as Instagram—optimized for real connection, not engagement metrics
  • Cooperative governance structures that scale to millions of users
  • Sustainable revenue models that don't depend on advertising or selling user data
  • Migration tools that make switching from billionaire platforms painless
  • Interoperability between co-op platforms so users aren't locked in

Skills Needed

Mobile developers (React Native, Swift, Kotlin) Backend engineers (distributed systems) UX/UI designers Co-op governance experts Community organizers Content moderation specialists Legal (co-op incorporation) Product managers

Commerce & Local Economy

The Problem

Amazon has hollowed out local economies. Independent bookstores, hardware shops, and artisans can't compete with a company that runs at a loss to crush competitors. Jeff Bezos spiked the Washington Post's endorsement of Harris, pays workers poverty wages, and spends millions busting unions. Every Prime subscription funds this.

What We're Building

City-based marketplace co-ops owned by local merchants. Imagine Amazon's convenience—search, browse, buy online, get delivery—but the merchants own the platform. Revenue stays in your city. No billionaire middleman taking a 40% cut.

What Already Exists

Bookshop.org

Online bookstore that supports independent bookshops. Has distributed over $30M to indie stores. Proves the model works for books.

Stocksy

Artist-owned stock photography co-op. Members earn 50-75% of sales (vs. industry standard of 15-45%). Co-op model, profitable.

REI

Consumer co-op with 23M+ members. $3.7B in revenue. Proves co-ops can compete at massive scale in retail.

Local food co-ops

Thousands of food co-ops across the US. Community-owned, locally sourced, worker-friendly. The OG model we're building on.

What Still Needs Building

  • A marketplace platform that's as easy to use as Amazon—search, buy, track, return
  • Logistics and delivery infrastructure that works for local merchants
  • Merchant onboarding tools that make joining the co-op simple
  • Payment processing that minimizes fees for small businesses
  • City-by-city launch playbooks so this can replicate everywhere

Skills Needed

E-commerce developers Logistics & operations UX/UI designers Local business organizers Payment systems engineers Marketing & growth Legal (co-op incorporation) Data & search engineers

Current Project: SF Marketplace Co-op

Starting in San Francisco with books and home goods. Local bookstores, artisans, and makers own the platform together. One city first, then the playbook goes open source for every city.
